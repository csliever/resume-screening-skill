# Resume Screening Rubric

Use this reference when the user wants a reusable hiring screen, a more rigorous score, or comparison across candidates.

## 100-Point Rubric

| Dimension | Weight | What to Look For |
|---|---:|---|
| Must-have skills and experience | 30 | Required tools, methods, years or depth of experience, direct evidence of hands-on work, certifications only when actually required. |
| Role responsibility match | 25 | Similar responsibilities, ownership level, project lifecycle exposure, stakeholder context, scope of execution. |
| Industry or business context | 15 | Relevant domain, customer type, regulatory context, product type, operational environment, market similarity. |
| Outcomes and impact | 15 | Metrics, shipped results, revenue/cost/efficiency impact, scale, quality of achievements, promotions or leadership signals. |
| Stability and stage fit | 10 | Tenure pattern, startup or enterprise fit, team size, growth trajectory, expected seniority. |
| Communication and resume clarity | 5 | Specificity, structure, evidence quality, clarity of responsibilities and achievements. |

## Scoring Guidance

- Award high scores only when resume evidence is explicit.
- Use partial credit for adjacent experience that is likely transferable.
- Use "unknown" rather than zero when the resume omits a point that could be clarified.
- Do not let nice-to-have strengths compensate for a failed hard gate unless the user says the gate is flexible.
- If the job description is vague, state the assumptions used for scoring.
- Do not use fixed education or years-of-experience tables unless the job requirement explicitly defines those thresholds. Score demonstrated responsibility, scope, and outcomes over credentials alone.
- Consider non-traditional backgrounds and career changes when the candidate provides strong evidence for the actual work required.

## Hard Gate Examples

Treat these as hard gates only if the job requirement clearly says they are mandatory:

- Required credential, license, degree, language, work authorization, location, travel ability, or shift availability.
- Required experience with a specific regulated domain.
- Required seniority level or minimum hands-on experience.
- Required technology when the role cannot be performed without it.

If evidence is missing, ask a targeted follow-up instead of rejecting automatically.

## Recommendation Labels

Use one of these labels:

- `建议面试`: Evidence shows direct fit or strong transferable fit, and risks are interview-checkable.
- `待定`: Fit depends on missing information, a hiring-manager preference, or one major risk.
- `不建议推进`: Hard-gate failure, weak evidence for core responsibilities, or large mismatch with role level.

## Candidate Comparison

When ranking multiple candidates:

1. Normalize all candidates against the same requirements and weights.
2. Keep hard-gate status visible even when the numeric score is high.
3. Prefer the candidate with stronger core requirement evidence over the candidate with more nice-to-have breadth.
4. Break ties using risk level, evidence specificity, and likely ramp-up speed.
5. Add a short applicant-pool summary when comparing three or more candidates, including common strengths, common gaps, and sourcing or interview-process recommendations.

## Follow-Up Question Patterns

Use questions like:

- "简历中提到 X，请说明你在其中负责的具体模块、决策权和最终结果。"
- "这个岗位要求 Y，你过往最接近的经历是什么？规模、周期和结果分别是什么？"
- "你对 Z 的经验在简历中没有体现，请补充最近一次实际使用场景。"
- "如果入职后需要在 N 周内完成某项任务，你会如何拆解和推进？"
